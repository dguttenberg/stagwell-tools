01 · Agentic OS

The Machine.

Built by Code and Theory · Launched CES 2026
Clients: Qualcomm, TIME, Stanley Black & Decker, T. Rowe Price, Henry Schein

The Machine is an AI operating system that unifies people, tools, and data to orchestrate more intelligent work. It ingests and acts on large volumes of private, secure signals — client data, Stagwell proprietary sources, world knowledge, and trusted third parties — and uses all of that to make work faster, smarter, and more connected to real consumer behavior.

It has its own interface and also runs inside tools people already use — Adobe, Slack, Teams — through purpose-built plugins called Mini-Machines. It is platform-agnostic and API-first.

The holdcos built AI on top of existing agency structures. The Machine is built around a different premise: that the workflow itself changes.

Strategy, creative, and production stop being sequential handoffs and become an orchestrated, continuously running system — where every brief informs an asset, every asset informs a buy, and the whole thing gets smarter over time.

What it actually does

Four live use cases.

Identifies positioning opportunities and cultural white space through AI-powered market analysis, then helps craft strategies that authentically connect with target audiences. Turns what used to be months of brand strategy work into a continuously updated, intelligence-driven process.
Creates precision-targeted content variations at scale — dozens of versions in the time it traditionally takes to produce one — personalized across channels, markets, and audience segments. The creative doesn't just get made faster; it gets made smarter, with each variation informed by real audience data.
Monitors cultural signals and trending moments in real time, then activates instantly with the right talent and influencers — from signal to live campaign in a fraction of traditional timelines. The window of relevance is shrinking; this is how you stay inside it.
Surfaces actionable insights from campaign data to continuously refine creative, optimize spend, and prove ROI — with reporting intelligence that updates assets in real time. Not a dashboard you check at the end of the quarter. A system that makes the work better while it's running.
02 · Data Infrastructure

Palantir + the ID Graph.

Partnership announced November 2025
The ID Graph underpins Stagwell's media and audience strategy tools

The ID Graph is Stagwell's proprietary identity infrastructure — a system that connects a person's various digital signals (devices, email addresses, behavioral data, location patterns) into a single unified profile. This is what makes it possible to recognize the same person across different channels without relying on third-party cookies.

It powers Stagwell's media planning, buying, and audience strategy tools — giving those products a first-party-grounded view of who the audience actually is.

Why Palantir matters

Palantir's Foundry platform was built to process extraordinarily complex, large-scale data problems — originally for defense and intelligence. The partnership brings that infrastructure to marketing: the ability to work across tens of millions of audience records with precision and privacy architecture most marketing data stacks can't handle.

Palantir essentially never partners with advertising companies. Their reference clients are governments and defense departments. The fact that they entered marketing at all — and chose Stagwell — is the signal.

Competitive honest take

Publicis (with Epsilon) and IPG (with Acxiom) have larger, more mature audience data businesses today. Stagwell's position isn't that it has the biggest data asset right now.

It's that the architecture being built with Palantir is designed for an AI-native era of targeting, not retrofitted from older infrastructure. That's a genuine technical distinction, not a marketing claim.

The privacy architecture uses differential privacy developed with Harvard's OpenDP project — the same framework the U.S. Census uses — allowing precise targeting without exposing individual-level data.

03 · Software Platform

The Marketing Cloud.

Stagwell's centralized technology function · 34% organic growth in 2025
Software products purpose-built for marketing, available across the network and sold directly to clients

The Marketing Cloud builds software products for specific marketing problems and makes them available across all Stagwell agencies and directly to clients. Some products are sold as software subscriptions — clients pay a recurring fee to use the tool themselves rather than paying for agency hours. That model scales without adding headcount and gets better the more it's used.

One problem solved becomes a shared capability. The network gets smarter every time any part of it figures something out.

The Products

What's actually been built.

Tracks brand health for 1,000+ brands across 21 markets in real time — replacing quarterly research cycles with a live dashboard. Instead of waiting three months to find out how your brand is performing, you know now.
Uses AI to predict which journalists will cover a story before it's ever pitched. Turns PR from a spray-and-pray discipline into a precision-targeted one. The system learns from outcomes and gets sharper over time.
Scores a brand's entire creative library for predicted performance. Before you run something, you have a data-informed read on how it's likely to perform — across formats, channels, and audiences. Helps creative teams make better decisions, not just faster ones.
Assembly's media operating system connects 400+ data sources and runs planning, buying, and optimization, with the ID Graph providing the audience foundation underneath. This is the product where the data infrastructure and the software platform converge most directly.

One nuance worth knowing

Not all Marketing Cloud products share a data layer — STAGE AI and audience strategy tools connect to the ID Graph; others like SmartAssets and PRophet operate more independently. The shared dashboard gives users a unified experience across the suite. What could eventually connect the applications at a deeper level is The Machine.
